# Mediawise Solutions Brand Guidelines — Complete Package

## Overview

This folder contains the complete brand identity system for Mediawise Solutions. Use these guidelines to maintain consistency across all marketing, communications, design, and content.

## Documents Included

### 1. **BRAND_GUIDELINES.md** (Strategic Foundation)
The master brand document covering:
- Brand positioning and promise
- Target audience definition
- Color palette and visual identity specifications
- Brand voice and tone
- Key messages and positioning statements
- Service offerings communication
- Brand values and principles
- Do's and don'ts for brand representation

**Use this when:** Making strategic decisions, approving content, defining brand direction

### 2. **BRAND_VISUAL_STYLE.md** (Design System)
The visual design specification including:
- Color palette with hex codes and RGB values
- Typography system (headings, body, accent)
- Component styles (buttons, cards, icons)
- Layout and spacing guidelines
- Responsive design breakpoints
- Imagery and photography guidelines
- Accessibility and contrast requirements
- File formats and specifications

**Use this when:** Designing website pages, creating marketing materials, developing new layouts, building templates

### 3. **CONTENT_STYLE_GUIDE.md** (Writing Standards)
Guidelines for all written content:
- Core voice attributes and tone guidelines
- Preferred language and phrases
- Words to avoid and words to use
- Messaging frameworks (problem-solution, results-first, credentials)
- Sentence structure and punctuation
- Copywriting checklists for different content types
- Common phrases and formulas
- Before/after examples of good and bad copy

**Use this when:** Writing website copy, crafting emails, creating social content, developing case studies

## Key Brand Principles

### 1. Execution Over Strategy
- A strategy is only as good as its execution
- Focus on what Mitch has *done*, not what he recommends
- Show the systems and processes, not just the thinking

### 2. Results Over Recommendations
- Lead with measurable outcomes (revenue, efficiency, team performance)
- Include specific metrics and timelines
- Show business impact, not deliverables

### 3. Systems Over Heroes
- Emphasize repeatable, sustainable systems
- Built for long-term success beyond the engagement
- Processes that "keep running after I'm gone"

### 4. Transparency Over Positioning
- Be honest about fit and limitations
- Upfront about what we can and can't do
- No overselling or vague promises

### 5. Commercial Reality Over Corporate Theory
- Focus on what works in real business environments
- Practical solutions, not frameworks
- Rooted in a decade of execution

## Common Scenarios & How to Apply Guidelines

### Creating a New Landing Page

1. **Design:** Use color palette and typography from VISUAL_STYLE.md
2. **Headlines:** Ensure they're specific and benefit-focused (refer to CONTENT_STYLE_GUIDE.md)
3. **Copy:** Check messaging frameworks and tone (CONTENT_STYLE_GUIDE.md)
4. **Buttons:** Use teal accent color and button style (VISUAL_STYLE.md)
5. **Verify:** Check against brand voice principles (BRAND_GUIDELINES.md)

### Reaching Out to a Prospect

1. **Tone:** Reference email outreach checklist (CONTENT_STYLE_GUIDE.md)
2. **Message:** Use problem-solution or results-first framework
3. **Specificity:** Include relevant metrics or outcomes
4. **Personal:** From Mitch, not generic company outreach
5. **CTA:** Direct and simple ("let's talk," not "let's synergize")

### Writing Case Study Content

1. **Structure:** Use results-first framework (CONTENT_STYLE_GUIDE.md)
2. **Lead with:** Specific outcome (metric, achievement)
3. **Include:** Client context, approach, specific results, business impact
4. **Voice:** Direct, honest, personal (CONTENT_STYLE_GUIDE.md)
5. **Verify:** All outcomes are specific and quantified

### Designing Marketing Materials

1. **Colors:** Use approved palette (VISUAL_STYLE.md)
2. **Typography:** Follow heading and body guidelines
3. **Layout:** Generous whitespace, clean organization
4. **Copy:** Apply content style guidelines
5. **Imagery:** Professional, authentic (no stock photo feel)

## When You're Unsure

### Does this feel "on brand"?

Ask yourself:

1. **Specific?** — Can you point to concrete outcomes/examples?
2. **Honest?** — Am I overpromising or being transparent?
3. **Personal?** — Does this sound like Mitch or a faceless corporation?
4. **Results-focused?** — Is this about outcomes or deliverables?
5. **Direct?** — Can a 10th grader understand this without jargon?

If you can say "yes" to all of these, you're on brand.

## Maintaining Brand Consistency

### Regular Audits

- **Monthly:** Check website and marketing materials for consistency
- **Quarterly:** Review social media and email communications
- **Annually:** Full brand audit against guidelines

### Updating Guidelines

- Version guidelines when brand evolves
- Document reasons for changes
- Communicate updates to team
- Archive previous versions

### Common Consistency Issues

**Issue:** Copy sounds generic or corporate  
**Solution:** Apply word-choice guidelines and tone framework from CONTENT_STYLE_GUIDE.md

**Issue:** Results don't feel specific enough  
**Solution:** Add metrics, timelines, scope. Review results-first framework examples.

**Issue:** Design feels misaligned with brand  
**Solution:** Check color palette, typography, and spacing against VISUAL_STYLE.md

**Issue:** Mixed tone across materials  
**Solution:** Have team review CONTENT_STYLE_GUIDE.md and tone examples
