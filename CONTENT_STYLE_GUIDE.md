# Mediawise Solutions Content Style Guide

## Writing Principles

### Core Voice Attributes

1. **Direct** — Say what you mean clearly. No fluff, no jargon.
2. **Honest** — Be upfront about capabilities, limitations, and fit.
3. **Specific** — Use concrete examples, numbers, and outcomes (not generalities).
4. **Personal** — Write as Mitch, not as an anonymous corporation.
5. **Results-Focused** — Lead with what gets achieved, not what gets delivered.
6. **Professional** — Credible expertise backed by proven track record.

### Tone Guidelines

| Situation | Tone | Example |
|-----------|------|---------|
| Explaining services | Confident, practical | "I design workflows that actually get used." |
| Discussing results | Proud, specific | "Lifted CQ to #1 national ranking—not because of a slide deck." |
| Addressing problems | Empathetic, solutions-focused | "Most businesses leak time through broken processes." |
| About the company | Personal, humble | "I've spent a decade executing one." |
| Calls-to-action | Direct, inviting | "Get in touch and let's talk." |

## Language Guidelines

### Preferred Language

| Instead Of | Use | Reason |
|----------|-----|--------|
| "Provide solutions" | "Fix this" or "Build systems" | More direct and specific |
| "Best practices" | "What actually works" | More authentic and credible |
| "Optimize outcomes" | "Grow revenue" or "Fix the bottleneck" | Clearer business impact |
| "Leverage synergies" | "Work together on this" | Plain English, no jargon |
| "Comprehensive strategy" | "Built around your commercial outcomes" | Specific and tangible |
| "Thought leadership" | "Industry insight" or "A decade of experience" | More genuine and credible |
| "Synergistic alignment" | "Works together" or "Makes sense for you" | Clear and conversational |
| "Deliver value" | "Grow revenue", "Save time", "Fix the problem" | Specific outcomes |
| "Paradigm shift" | "A different approach" | Plain language |
| "Going forward" | "Next", "Moving ahead", "From here" | Clearer and lighter |

### Words to Avoid

- **Corporate jargon:** "leverage," "synergy," "paradigm," "circle back," "touch base," "take offline"
- **Vague terms:** "solutions," "innovative," "dynamic," "cutting-edge," "powerful"
- **Overblown language:** "revolutionary," "transformation," "game-changer," "disruption"
- **Passive voice:** Avoid where possible. Use active voice instead.
- **Hedging language:** "might," "could," "sort of," "kind of" (be confident)

### Words to Use

- **Specific actions:** "Build," "fix," "design," "coach," "audit," "improve"
- **Outcome-driven:** "Revenue," "growth," "efficiency," "systems," "results"
- **Honest qualifiers:** "I've," "we've," "proven," "actual," "real," "specific"
- **Active verbs:** "Lead," "manage," "deliver," "execute," "create"

## Messaging Frameworks

### Problem-Solution Format

**Structure:**
1. Name the problem (what most businesses experience)
2. Show impact (why it matters)
3. Explain the root cause (not obvious, but fixable)
4. State the solution (what we do differently)

**Example:**
"Most businesses leak time through broken processes. Sales forecasts get made in spreadsheets. Key decisions are made on outdated information. The problem isn't people—it's structure. I design workflows that fix this."

### Results-First Format

**Structure:**
1. Lead with specific outcome (number, metric, achievement)
2. Context (what was managed, the scope)
3. How it was done (the approach, briefly)
4. Why it matters (business impact)

**Example:**
"#1 National Revenue Ranking — SCA Regional Markets FY25. Managed a $7M annual portfolio across three regional markets. Built the campaign strategy, performance systems, and team culture that lifted CQ to the top-ranked regional operation in Australia."

### Credentials Format

**Structure:**
1. Title/Role + Organization
2. Scale/Scope (portfolio, team, market)
3. Key Achievement (metric or outcome)
4. Relevant skill/certification

**Example:**
"General Sales Manager — Southern Cross Austereo
$7M portfolio · Team of 12 · #1 national revenue ranking FY25"

## Sentence Structure

### Preferred Patterns

✓ **GOOD:** Short, direct sentences  
"I help regional SMEs grow revenue. I build marketing strategy. I fix broken systems."

✓ **GOOD:** Specific nouns and verbs  
"Designed and launched a reactivation initiative. Built Salesforce workflows. Coached the team through the transition."

✓ **GOOD:** Active voice  
"I identified the highest-leverage opportunities."

✗ **AVOID:** Long, complex sentences  
"Through the implementation of comprehensive, strategically-aligned, synergistic solutions, we aim to potentially optimize outcomes."

✗ **AVOID:** Passive voice  
"Opportunities were identified. Systems were built. Results were achieved."

✗ **AVOID:** Nested qualifications  
"While some consultants might, in certain circumstances, provide what could be considered strategic recommendations that may or may not..."

### Punctuation & Style

- Use sentence fragments for impact (sparingly): "Most consultants deliver a strategy and leave. I've spent a decade executing one."
- Use em dashes for emphasis: "A decade of execution—not just strategy."
- Use semicolons to connect related ideas: "Strategy execution requires structure; I've built it at scale."
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences) for scannability
- Use bullet points for lists (don't bury in prose)
- Bold key words or phrases for emphasis

## Copywriting Checklists

### Website Page Copy

- [ ] Headline answers "why should I care?" (business impact)
- [ ] Lead paragraph is punchy (1-2 sentences)
- [ ] Copy uses active voice throughout
- [ ] Every sentence has purpose (no fluff)
- [ ] Specific numbers, metrics, outcomes included
- [ ] Calls-to-action are clear and direct
- [ ] Jargon has been removed and replaced
- [ ] Tone matches brand voice (direct, honest, results-focused)
- [ ] Credentials and proof points are specific
- [ ] Copy has been read aloud (sounds natural, conversational)

### Email Outreach

- [ ] Subject line is specific to recipient's business
- [ ] First sentence shows you understand their situation
- [ ] Body is short (3-5 sentences maximum)
- [ ] States what you noticed (their business, situation, opportunity)
- [ ] Proposes next step (conversation, brief call)
- [ ] From line is personal (Mitch Harris, not "Mediawise Team")
- [ ] Tone is conversational, not corporate
- [ ] No "dear sir/madam" or generic openings
- [ ] CTA is simple and clear ("Let's talk" not "Let's synergize")

### Social Media / LinkedIn

- [ ] Post is about insight, not self-promotion
- [ ] Relevant to target audience's challenges
- [ ] Includes specific example or metric
- [ ] Voice is personal and conversational
- [ ] Length is appropriate for platform (LinkedIn: 3-5 short paragraphs)
- [ ] Ends with a genuine question or thought-provoking statement
- [ ] Hashtags are minimal and relevant (#revenue, #strategy, not #blessed)
- [ ] Professional tone balanced with personality

### Case Studies / Results

- [ ] Headline leads with outcome (metric, achievement)
- [ ] Client situation/challenge is clearly stated
- [ ] Specific tactics and approach explained
- [ ] Results are quantified (numbers, percentages, timelines)
- [ ] Impact on business is clear (revenue, efficiency, team)
- [ ] Shows systems/processes built to sustain results
- [ ] Quote from client (if possible) is specific
- [ ] Learning or approach is transferable to reader

## Common Phrases & Formulas

### Opening Statements

- "I help [target] build [outcome] without [constraint]."
- "Most [type of business] struggle with [problem]. The issue is usually [root cause], not [common misconception]."
- "[Number] years of [experience] taught me that [insight]."

### Describing Your Approach

- "I embed into your business—not as an external advisor."
- "I own the outcomes, not just make recommendations."
- "I design systems that keep running after I'm gone."
- "My focus is [outcome], not [distraction]."

### Explaining Results

- "[Metric] because of [cause], not [common assumption]."
- "Built the [specific system/strategy] that [result]."
- "Lifted [subject] to [achievement]—not because of a slide deck, but because of [reason]."

### Calls to Action

- "Get in touch and let's talk."
- "Let's have a conversation about whether I can help."
- "If you think [situation applies], let me know."
- "Interested in exploring this further? Get in touch."

### Closing Phrases

- "Direct, honest, and focused on outcomes over outputs."
- "No obligation—just a conversation."
- "Straight talk, no pitch deck."
- "Response within one business day."

## Formatting & Presentation

### Headers & Subheaders

- Use descriptive, benefit-focused headers
- All caps for main headings: "REVENUE STRATEGY THAT MOVES"
- Title case for subheadings: "What I Deliver"
- Short and scannable (under 7 words preferred)

### Lists & Bullets

**Use bullets for:**
- Features or benefits
- Steps in a process
- Key points to remember
- Credentials or achievements

**Use numbers for:**
- Processes (step 1, step 2)
- Rankings or priorities
- Timelines or sequences

**Format:**
- Start with strong verb or noun
- Keep items parallel in structure
- Under 15 words per item
- No period at end of short items (use period for sentences)

### Emphasis & Styling

- **Bold** for key outcomes or important words
- *Italics* for emphasis (use sparingly)
- Gold color for brand accent words (visual design)
- ALL CAPS for headlines only

### Line Length & White Space

- Max width of 600px for body text
- Line-height of 1.6 for readability
- Ample margins and white space
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences)
- Breathing room between sections

## Review Checklist

Before publishing any content, ask:

1. **Is it specific?** (Does it include numbers, examples, or concrete details?)
2. **Is it honest?** (Am I overpromising or glossing over limitations?)
3. **Is it direct?** (Can a 10th grader understand this? Is there jargon?)
4. **Is it about outcomes?** (What gets achieved, not what gets delivered?)
5. **Does it sound like Mitch?** (Personal, confident, based on real experience?)
6. **Would a potential client want to talk to me?** (Does it inspire confidence?)
7. **Have I removed fluff?** (Every sentence has purpose?)

If you can't say "yes" to all of these, revise.

## Brand Voice Examples

### ✓ GOOD Examples

**Headline:**  
"Revenue Strategy That Moves"  
*Why: Direct, specific, benefit-focused*

**About:**  
"Most consultants deliver a strategy and leave. I've spent a decade executing one."  
*Why: Honest comparison, personal, results-focused*

**Service Description:**  
"I design and build automation and reporting workflows that fix this—tools you'll actually use."  
*Why: Specific outcome, implies understanding of common problem*

**Results:**  
"Designed and launched a structured reactivation initiative targeting lapsed clients. Built the approach, trained the team, and drove measurable incremental revenue within 60 days of launch."  
*Why: Specific timeline, actionable process, measurable outcome*

**CTA:**  
"Get in touch and we can have a straight conversation about whether I can help."  
*Why: Direct, honest about fit, personal*

### ✗ Avoid Examples

**Headline:**  
"Innovative Strategic Solutions for Business Growth"  
*Why: Vague, jargon-heavy, no specific benefit*

**About:**  
"Leveraging decades of experience to drive transformational change through synergistic partnerships."  
*Why: Jargon, passive voice, no credible specifics*

**Service Description:**  
"We provide comprehensive marketing and business improvement solutions tailored to your unique needs."  
*Why: Vague, generic, could apply to anyone*

**Results:**  
"Delivered best practices and strategic recommendations that enabled growth initiatives."  
*Why: No specific outcomes, passive voice, empty terms*

**CTA:**  
"Reach out to discuss how we can drive results together."  
*Why: Generic, corporate, impersonal*
