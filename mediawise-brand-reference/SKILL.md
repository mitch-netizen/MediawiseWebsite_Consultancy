## Brand Voice & Tone

### Core Attributes
- **Direct** — Say what you mean clearly. No fluff, no jargon.
- **Honest** — Be upfront about capabilities, fit, and limitations.
- **Specific** — Use concrete examples, numbers, outcomes (not generalities).
- **Personal** — Write as Mitch, not as an anonymous corporation.
- **Results-focused** — Lead with what gets achieved, not what gets delivered.
- **Professional** — Credible expertise backed by proven track record.

### Tone Guidelines

| Situation | Tone | Example |
|-----------|------|---------|
| Explaining services | Confident, practical | "I design workflows that actually get used." |
| Discussing results | Proud, specific | "Lifted CQ to #1 national ranking—not because of a slide deck." |
| Addressing problems | Empathetic, solutions-focused | "Most businesses leak time through broken processes." |
| About the company | Personal, humble | "I've spent a decade executing one." |
| Calls-to-action | Direct, inviting | "Get in touch and let's talk." |

### Language Rules

**Preferred language:**
- "Build," "fix," "design," "coach," "audit," "improve" (specific actions)
- "Revenue," "growth," "efficiency," "systems," "results" (outcomes)
- "Proven," "actual," "real," "specific" (honest qualifiers)
- Active voice: "I lead," not "leadership is provided"

**Avoid:**
- Corporate jargon: "leverage," "synergy," "paradigm," "circle back," "touch base"
- Vague terms: "solutions," "innovative," "dynamic," "cutting-edge," "powerful"
- Overblown language: "revolutionary," "transformation," "game-changer," "disruption"
- Passive voice and hedging: "might," "could," "sort of," "kind of"

### Example Opening Statements

- "I help [target] build [outcome] without [constraint]."
- "Most [type of business] struggle with [problem]. The issue is usually [root cause], not [common misconception]."
- "[Number] years of [experience] taught me that [insight]."

## Typography

### Heading Styles
- **H1 (Main Headlines):** 48pt+, bold, UPPERCASE, white text on dark background
- **H2 (Section Headings):** 36pt, bold, UPPERCASE, white text
- **H3 (Subsections):** 24pt, bold, uppercase or Title Case, white text

### Body Text
- **Font:** Clean, modern sans-serif (e.g., Inter, Helvetica Neue, or system sans-serif)
- **Size:** 16pt (desktop), 14pt (mobile)
- **Color:** Light gray or white text on dark background
- **Line-height:** 1.6 for readability
- **Max width:** 600px for optimal readability

### Accent Text
- Style: White text with Gold (#d8c38a) underline
- Usage: Emphasize key words or phrases within body text

## Copy Guidelines

### Messaging Frameworks

**Problem-Solution Format:**
1. Name the problem (what most businesses experience)
2. Show impact (why it matters)
3. Explain root cause (not obvious, but fixable)
4. State the solution (what we do differently)

**Results-First Format:**
1. Lead with specific outcome (number, metric, achievement)
2. Context (what was managed, the scope)
3. How it was done (the approach, briefly)
4. Why it matters (business impact)

**Credentials Format:**
1. Title/Role + Organization
2. Scale/Scope (portfolio, team, market)
3. Key Achievement (metric or outcome)
4. Relevant skill/certification

### Writing Checklist

Before publishing ANY content, verify:
- [ ] Is it specific? (Numbers, examples, concrete details?)
- [ ] Is it honest? (Am I overpromising or glossing over limitations?)
- [ ] Is it direct? (No jargon? 10th grader could understand?)
- [ ] Is it about outcomes? (What gets achieved, not what gets delivered?)
- [ ] Does it sound like Mitch? (Personal, confident, based on real experience?)
- [ ] Would a potential client want to talk? (Does it inspire confidence?)
- [ ] Have I removed fluff? (Every sentence has purpose?)
